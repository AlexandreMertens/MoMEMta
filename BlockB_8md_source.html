1 # Block B description
2 Note: Block B, also referred as final block (FB), and class in the MadWeight world.
3 Description inspired on MadWeight.
4 
5 This block corresponds to an initial state with Bjorken fractions \f$q_1\f$ and \f$q_2\f$,
6 and a final state with a visible particle with momenta \f$p_2\f$, and an invisible particle
7 with momenta \f$p_1\f$, both coming from a resonance with mass \f$s_{12}\f$. Extra radiation (ISR) it is also allowed.
8 
9 The goal of this Block is to address the change of variables needed to pass from the standard phase-space
10 parametrization to the \f$\frac{1}{4\pi E_1} ds_{12} \times J\f$ parametrization. Per integration point
11 in CUBA, this Block outputs the value of the jacobian, J, and the four momenta of the invisible particle,
12 \f$p_1\f$. The system of equations needed to compute \f$p_1\f$ is described below.
13 
14 
15 ### Change of variables
16 
17 From the standard phase-space parametrisation:
18 
19 \f$dq_1 dq_2\frac{d^3 p_1}{(2\pi)^3 2E_1}(2\pi)^4 \delta^4 (P_{in}-P_{fin})\f$
20 
21 where \f$P_{in} and P_{fin)\f$ are the total four-momenta in the initial and final states, to the
22 following parametrisation:
23 
24 \f$\frac{1}{4\pi E_1} ds_{12} \times J\f$
25 
26 where the jacobian, J, is given by:
27 
28 \f$J = \frac{E_1}{s} |p_{2z} E_1 - E_2 p_{1z}|^{-1}\f$
29 
30 ### Parameters
31 
32 - Collision energy.
33 
34 ### Inputs
35 
36 - Visible particle, with four momenta \f$p_2\f$
37 - The mass \f$s_{12}\f$ as output from the Flatter module.
38 
39 ### Outputs
40 
41 - Invisible particle, with four momenta \f$p_1\f$ (up to two solutions possible)
42 - Jacobian, one per solution.

44 ### System of equations to compute \f$p_1\f$
45 
46 The integrator throws random points in the mass (\f$s_{12}\f$) while \f$p_2\f$ is a known quantity. The equations to
47 compute \f$p_1\f$ are:
48 
49  1. \f$(p_1 + p_2)^2 = s_{12} = M_{1}^{2} + M_{2}^2 + 2 E_1 E_2 + 2 p_{1x}p_{2x} + 2p_{1y}p_{2y} + p_{1z}p_{2z}\f$
50  2. \f$p_{1x} = - p_{Tx}\f$ #Coming from \f$p_{T}^{\nu} = -p_{T}^{visible} = - (p_2 + ISR)\f$
51  3. \f$p_{1y} = - p_{Ty}\f$ #Coming from \f$p_{T}^{\nu} = -p_{T}^{visible} = - (p_1 + ISR)\f$
52  4. \f$M_1 = 0 \to E_{1}^2 = p_{1x}^2 + p_{1y}^2 + p_{1z}^2\f$
53 
54 Using the values of \f$p_{1x}, p_{1y}\f$ from equations (2) and (3), equation (1) can be written as \f$p_{1z} = A - B E_1\f$. Where A and B are:
55 
56 - \f$A = \frac{s_{12} - M_{2}^2 + 2(p_{Tx}p_{2x} + p_{Ty}p_{2y})}{2 p_{2z}}\f$
57 - \f$B = \frac{E_2}{p_{2z}}\f$
58 
59 Finally equation (4) can be written as:
60 
61 - \f$(1 - B) E_{1}^2 + 2AB E_1 - C = 0\f$, where \f$C = p_{Tx}^{2} + p_{Ty}^{2}\f$
62 
63 Each solution of the quadratic equation with a positive value of $$E_1\f$ is taken.
